Plural - when there's more than one

So far you’ve only been introduced to nouns in the singular, i.e. nouns referring to only one person, place, thing or concept. In this lesson, you are going to learn how to form the plural in Spanish.

Plural of definite and indefinite articles

You’ve learned that el and la are the singular forms of the definite articles, while un and una are the singular forms of the indefinite articles. Their corresponding plural forms are as follows:

Pluralizing nouns

Pluralizing nouns in Spanish is quite simple. You’ll need to look at the last letter of the noun:

If it’s a vowel (-a, -e, -i, -o, -u), just add -s to the end of the noun.

Plural

El hombre

Los hombres

La señora

Las señoras

Un hermano

Unos hermanos

Unas niñas

The man

Los hombres

The men

La señora

The lady

The ladies

A brother

Some brothers

A girl

Unas niñas

Some girls

If the words ends in -z, drop the z and add -ces to the end of the noun.

Pluralizing adjectives

Let’s look at a few examples, it’s really easy to get because they are exactly the same rules as you have studied before to make nouns plural.

For adjectives that end in a vowel (-a, -e, -i, -o, -u) just add -s to the end.

Singular

Plural

bueno

buenos

buena

buenas

grande

grandes

El niño bueno

The good boy

Los niños buenos

The good boys

La niña buena

The good girl

Las niñas buenas

The good girls

Una casa grande

A big house

Unas casas grandes

Some big houses

For adjectives that end in -z: just drop the z and add -ces.

Singular

Plural

feliz

felices

El hombre feliz 

The happy man

Los hombres felices

The happy men

La niña feliz

The happy girl

Las niñas felices

The happy girls

For adjectives that end in any other letter (consonant), just add -es to the end.

Singular

Plural

grises

La casa gris

The gray house

Las casas grises

The gray houses

El libro difícil

The difficult book

The difficult books

This doesn’t happen in English… but it makes sense: articles, nouns and adjectives are related to each other; one refers to the other! So, they should all match in gender and number (masculine/feminine, singular/plural). It’ll become more intuitive as time goes on.
