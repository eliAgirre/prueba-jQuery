Use tú or usted to address to a single person and vosotros or  ustedes (formal) to address two or more people.

Nosotras (we) and vosotras (you) are feminine and are used to refer to two or more women.

Ser

Now, let’s study the verb ser (to be). In order to use the verb ser, you need to conjugate it. This simply means that according to the personal pronoun used, the verb will change into different forms.

You will now examine how to conjugate the verb ser in the simple present tense:

Note: Verb ser - present simple

Notice that usted and ustedes is used in formal situations only. In informal situations the personal pronoun tú or vosotros/vosotras is used instead.

Notice that different words translated as you and use different forms: tú eres, usted es, vosotros sois, ustedes son.

This verb is used to talk about characteristics such as: name, nationality, occupation, color or physical appearances to name just a few. Have a look at the following examples:

Yo soy Juan 

I am Juan

Tú eres italiano 

You are Italian

Él es estadounidense 

He is American

Ellas son abogadas 

They are lawyers

Nosotros somos mecánicos 

We are mechanics

El perro es negro 

The dog is black

Ellos son verdes 

They are green

Ustedes son bajos 

You are short

Vosotros sois felices

You are happy

Él es alto

He is tall

Ella es alta 

She is tall

Professions

When we talk about professions in Spanish, we don’t need an article:

[  Ella es enfermera  ::  She is a nurse

[  Él es carpintero  ::  He is a carpenter

[  Nosotros somos profesores  ::  We are teachers

You have two ways to express your nationality: use de followed by a country or you use your nationality.

 

Yo soy de México 

I am from Mexico

Yo soy mexicano 

I am Mexican

Tú eres de Italia 

You are from Italy

Tú eres italiano 

You are Italian

Nosotras somos de Alemania

We are from Germany

Nosotros somos alemanes 

We are German

Use capitals for country names but not for nationalities.

     

To be

To be has two different translations in Spanish, the verb ser and the verb estar. They are both translated in English as to be. In this lesson we have covered ser and later on you will study the differences between ser and estar.
